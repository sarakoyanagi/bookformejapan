The Problem

Japan's gatekeepers
speak only Japanese.

The most coveted restaurants, traditional inns, and cultural experiences in Japan have no English website, no online reservation form, and no email. The only way in is a phone call — in Japanese.

📞
Phone-only reservations
Thousands of the best small restaurants, kappo counters, and ryokan accept bookings exclusively by phone. No app, no DM, no Google Forms.
🏆
Sumo & cultural events
Most sumo seats are pre-sold through JSA fan club lotteries — closed to overseas visitors. Popular days sell out in minutes on release.
🚫
The no-show shadow
Many establishments have quietly stopped accepting foreign guests after repeated no-shows. Trust is broken. Getting in now requires someone to vouch for you.
